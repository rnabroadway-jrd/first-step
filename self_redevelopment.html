Guide to Self Redevelopment

A structured breakdown of empowering the society to act as its own developer, maximizing member benefits while avoiding builder dependencies.

1

Member Consent & SGM Approval

Self-redevelopment begins with uniting the members. A formal Special General Meeting (SGM) must be convened to secure the mandated consent resolving to undertake self-redevelopment.

  • SGM Resolution: A formal resolution must be passed favoring self-redevelopment over traditional builder-led redevelopment.
  • Majority Required: A minimum of 51% (or as per prevailing state rules) of members must explicitly provide their consent.
  • Government Incentives: Adopting self-redevelopment unlocks specific concessions like stamp duty rebates, extra FSI (depending on state grs), and lower GST.
2

Appointing the Professional Core Team

Because the society acts as the developer, it must hire top-tier professionals (Architects, Legal Advisors, and PMCs) to execute the technical and compliance work.

  • Architect / PMC: Prepares the project feasibility report, layouts, estimates construction costs, and manages subsequent tenders.
  • Legal Advisor: Ensures title clearance, navigates state regulations, and drafts member agreements and contractor contracts.
  • Financial Consultant: Handles loan syndication, tax structuring (GST, Income Tax), and manages the escrow account for cash flow.
3

Securing Project Finance

Unlike conventional redevelopment where a builder brings capital, the society procures project funding from approved cooperative banks or NBFCs specifically dedicated for self-redevelopment.

  • Funding Source: Procure a construction loan from MHADA-approved banks (e.g., MDCC Bank). Members' personal properties securely held as primary collateral.
  • Sales Revenue: The loan is repaid via phased sales of the extra "saleable" flats built through the allowable FSI.
  • Surplus Profits: Any surplus generated after loan repayment belongs entirely to the society / members, drastically increasing corpora funds.
4

Design, Approvals & RERA Registration

The appointed Architect secures all necessary municipal permissions, Intimation of Disapproval (IOD), and finally, lists the project under MahaRERA.

  • Layout Finalization: The architect finalizes building plans incorporating amenities chosen directly by society members.
  • Civic Approvals: Securing IOD, Commencement Certificate (CC), and No Objection Certificates (Fire, Water, Aviation, etc).
  • MahaRERA: Even under self-redevelopment, the project must be registered under RERA to permit the legal sale of new inventory.
5

Hiring a Civil Contractor

The PMC floats tenders to hire an experienced, reputable civil construction company that executes the build under strict timeline contracts.

  • Lump-Sum Contract: Society typically opts for a fixed-price contract, transferring material / labor inflation risks to the contractor.
  • Quality Control: The society’s Architect and PMC act as strict auditors, ensuring high-quality construction materials are used.
  • Escrow Payments: Payments to the contractor are released strictly based on achieved completion milestones verified by the PMC.
6

Execution, OC & Handover

Members vacate properties upon receiving advance rent. Construction is completed, extra flats are sold, bank loan is cleared, and Occupation Certificate (OC) is received.

  • Vacation & Demolition: Members receive transit rent via project funding before handing over keys for demolition.
  • Marketing & Sales: A professional real estate agency is usually retained to rapidly market and sell the new open inventory flats.
  • Possession: Procurement of the final Occupation Certificate (OC). Members take possession of their significantly larger, upgraded homes.
