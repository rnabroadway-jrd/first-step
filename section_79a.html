Understanding Section 79A

A simplified breakdown of the guidelines governing the redevelopment of Co-operative Housing Societies in Maharashtra.

1

Convene a Special General Meeting (SGM)

The redevelopment process kicks off by calling an SGM where a preliminary decision is taken. It requires members to agree on exploring redevelopment as a path forward.

  • Application: Not less than 20% of the members submit an application to convene an SGM.
  • Timeline: The Managing Committee organizes the SGM within 1 month.
  • Quorum: Quorum required is 2/3rd of the total members of the society.
  • Resolution: A preliminary decision is passed to proceed with redevelopment, subject to feasible terms.
2

Appointment of a PMC (Project Management Consultant)

An experienced architect or PMC is officially appointed from an approved panel. Their role is to safely guide the society through technical, legal, and financial decisions transparently.

  • Shortlisting: The Society shortlists at least 3 PMCs/Architects from the government-approved panel.
  • Selection SGM: Another SGM is called to select the final PMC based on presentations and competitive quotations.
  • PMC Role: The appointed PMC will advise on allowable FSI, TDR, corpus fund formulas, rent allowances, and structural blueprints.
3

Project Feasibility Report

The PMC creates a detailed Project Feasibility Report outlining available FSI, estimated costs, corpus, member benefits, and expected timelines in accordance with current norms.

  • Surveying: The PMC surveys the land, existing structures, and societal title deeds.
  • Calculations: They calculate maximum permissible FSI, fungible FSI, and potential commercial value of the new plot.
  • Transparency: The report is circulated to all members digitally and physically for transparency before tendering starts.
  • Validation: Valid suggestions and feedback from members are incorporated into the final finalized report.
4

Inviting Tenders

A transparent tendering process is initiated. Verified developers submit their operational and financial bids based on the requirements detailed in the Feasibility Report.

  • Tender Document: The PMC drafts the document ensuring society expectations (budget, design) are strictly met.
  • Public Notice: Advertisements are placed in leading daily newspapers inviting competitive bids from developers.
  • Evaluation: Developers are evaluated on financial soundness, successful past project track record, and RERA standings.
  • Comparative Chart: A transparent comparative chart of all bids is prepared by the PMC to present to the society.
5

Selection of Developer via SGM

A final SGM is held in the presence of an authorized representative of the Registrar. A minimum required majority consensus must vote to select the most suitable developer.

  • Registrar Officer: The Registrar appoints an authorized officer to oversee the transparency of the SGM.
  • Video Recording: Video recording of the entire proceeding is mandatory for state compliance.
  • Majority Vote: A minimum of 51% of total members must approve the selected developer via majority vote.
  • Approval NOC: The resolution is minuted, signed, and submitted to the Registrar's office for a No Objection Certificate (NOC).
6

Signing of Development Agreement

The Society and the chosen Developer execute a formal Development Agreement (DA) detailing roles, timelines, penalties, and obligations, ensuring complete legal protection for the members.

  • Legal Vetting: The draft DA is meticulously prepared and legally vetted by independent legal consultants.
  • Document Details: The DA specifies construction timelines, rent compensations, corpus fund schedules, and bank guarantees.
  • PAAA Execution: Members sign separate agreements (Permanent Alternate Accommodation Agreement) locking in their specific flat dimensions.
  • Transition: Once properly vacated, the developer proceeds with demolition and construction under constant PMC scrutiny.
